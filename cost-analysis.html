Cost of a QC Check — In‑Depth

BB QC Labs · how much each scan actually costs, and why · generated 16 Jun 2026

Every scan spends money in two places: Claude vision (reads every frame for text/QC) and ElevenLabs Scribe (transcribes the voiceover for the audio cross‑check). There is no monthly platform fee — you pay per scan, drawn from your Anthropic and ElevenLabs credit. This document breaks down the exact math the app uses, real per‑reel costs, and the levers that move the number.

The one‑line answer. A typical 30‑second vertical reel costs about $0.10 on Urgent and about $0.70 on Deep Audit. Audio transcription adds well under a cent. Cost scales with length × frame rate × resolution — image tokens, not instructions, dominate the bill.

2. Where the money goes: image tokens dominate

The QC engine samples the video into still frames and sends them to Claude in small batches. The frames are ~95% of the cost. The instruction block (the big QC ruleset) is sent once and then served from cache at ~10% price on every later batch, so it barely registers. That means the three real cost levers are:

  • Length — more seconds → more frames.
  • Frame rate — Urgent samples 1 fps; Deep samples 2 fps (twice the frames).
  • Resolution — a 1080×1920 reel is ~2,765 tokens/frame; a 1280×720 clip is ~1,229. The app caps width at 1600px.

6. How to spend less

  • Default to Urgent. It catches the objective text errors (typos, grammar, safe‑zones) at ~1/7th the cost of Deep. Reserve Deep for final pre‑delivery review.
  • Trim before scanning. Cost is linear in length — QC the cut, not the raw export.
  • Resolution matters. A 1080‑wide reel costs ~2.2× a 720‑wide one per frame. The app already caps width at 1600px.
  • The instruction block is cached — back‑to‑back scans in the same session reuse it, so you pay the ~1.25× cache‑write premium once, not per scan.
  • Teach it once. The "Learn" memory and caption‑only scope cut false positives — fewer junk findings means slightly less output spend and far less of your review time.

7. The exact formula (for the record)

frames = clamp(8 … 600, ceil(duration_sec × fps)) tokens/frame = round(min(width,1600) × scaledHeight / 750) tech_batches = ceil(frames / 4) # 4 frames per API call calls = tech_batches (+1 creative pass on Deep) instruction = 2600 × (1 + 0.1 × (calls−1)) # 1 cache write + cache reads input_tokens = instruction + (frames + creativeFrames) × tokens/frame output_tokens = calls × 350 cost = input/1e6 × price_in + output/1e6 × price_out audio_cost = (duration_sec / 3600) × $0.40

Worked example — Urgent, 30s vertical reel: 30 frames → 8 calls → input ≈ 87,370 tok × $1 = $0.087; output 2,800 tok × $5 = $0.014; audio $0.003 → ≈ $0.10. This is exactly what the cost calculator on the Review screen shows before you start a scan.

Bottom line. Routine QC on short social reels runs a few cents to ~$0.20 a scan on Urgent. A full creative Deep Audit on a minute‑long vertical reel tops out around $1.30. For a shop doing ~100 reels a month, expect a $10–$70/month Claude bill depending on how often you run Deep, plus pennies for audio.
